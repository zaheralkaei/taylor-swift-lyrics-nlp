# Sentiment & emotion analysis — summary

Generated by `python analyze/sentiment.py` on 244 songs (CoTS v1.4 corpus).
Full per-song CSV: `reports/sentiment_per_song.csv` (regenerable, gitignored).

## Models used

| Model | Type | Output |
|-------|------|--------|
| VADER | Rule-based lexicon | neg/neu/pos + compound [-1,1] |
| TextBlob | Rule-based lexicon | polarity [-1,1] + subjectivity [0,1] |
| DistilBERT (SST-2 fine-tune) | Neural classifier | positive/negative probability |
| DistilRoBERTa (j-hartmann/emotion) | Neural classifier | 7-way emotion distribution |

## Overall sentiment (244 songs)

| Model | Mean | Median | Min | Max |
|-------|------|--------|-----|-----|
| VADER compound | +0.450 | +0.970 | -1.000 | +1.000 |
| TextBlob polarity | +0.097 | +0.102 | -0.625 | +0.575 |
| DistilBERT pos prob | +0.327 | +0.017 | +0.000 | +0.999 |

**Reading**: VADER is systematically optimistic (median +0.97 — it catches words like
"love", "beautiful", "kiss" but ignores surrounding context). DistilBERT is polarised
(median 0.02 — most songs push strongly positive OR strongly negative, little middle ground).
TextBlob is the most calibrated, with values clustered around zero.

## Caveats on the emotion model

The DistilRoBERTa emotion model was trained on short-form text (reviews, dialogue,
social-media style). Song lyrics are abstract, full of metaphor, and often deliberately
understate or invert emotion. Across all 244 songs the model's top-1 emotion is rarely
confident, and the means for each emotion class are low (e.g. mean `joy` = 0.052).
Take per-song emotion scores as rough indicators, not ground truth.
