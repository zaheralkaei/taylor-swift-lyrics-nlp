# Sentiment analysis — summary

Generated by `python analyze/sentiment.py` on 244 songs (CoTS v1.4 corpus).
Full per-song CSV: `reports/sentiment_per_song.csv` (regenerable, gitignored).

## Models used

| Model | Type | Output |
|-------|------|--------|
| VADER | Rule-based lexicon | neg/neu/pos + compound [-1,1] |
| TextBlob | Rule-based lexicon | polarity [-1,1] + subjectivity [0,1] |
| DistilBERT (SST-2 fine-tune) | Neural classifier | positive/negative probability |

Note: an earlier version of this pipeline also ran a 7-way emotion
classifier (j-hartmann/emotion-english-distilroberta-base). It was
removed because the model is trained on short-form reviews/dialogue
and produces near-uniform distributions on abstract lyrical text —
not a reliable signal. DistilBERT transfers well to lyrics.

**Honest framing**: sentiment analysis on lyrics is fundamentally hard.
Songs use metaphor, negation reversals, and quoted irony (e.g.
'I knew you were trouble when you walked in' — negative words
expressing a complex non-negative feeling). The pretrained DistilBERT
model was fine-tuned on SST-2 (movie reviews), not lyrics; its
output is a 'positive/negative' score relative to that training
distribution. We treat it as a useful relative signal across songs,
not a ground-truth emotional reading.

## Overall sentiment (244 songs)

| Model | Mean | Median | Min | Max |
|-------|------|--------|-----|-----|
| VADER compound | +0.464 | +0.970 | -1.000 | +1.000 |
| TextBlob polarity | +0.099 | +0.102 | -0.625 | +0.575 |
| DistilBERT pos prob | +0.335 | +0.016 | +0.000 | +0.999 |

**Reading**: VADER is systematically optimistic (median +0.97 — it catches
words like "love", "beautiful", "kiss" but ignores surrounding context).
DistilBERT is polarised (median 0.02 — most songs push strongly positive
OR strongly negative, little middle ground). TextBlob is the most
calibrated, with values clustered around zero.

- `The Way I Loved You` (Fearless) — bert_pos=0.999

**Caveat on extremes**: DistilBERT's outputs are NEARLY BIMODAL on this
corpus, not on a continuous scale:
- 135/244 songs (55%) get bert_pos < 0.05 (model is confident negative)
- 48/244 songs (20%) get bert_pos > 0.95 (model is confident positive)
- Only 61/244 songs (25%) sit in the uncertain middle (0.1-0.9)

The 'median 0.02' I previously cited wasn't quite right — it's not
'mostly low with some highs', it's 'two peaks, one at 0 and one at 1'.
Per-album means are therefore mostly a measure of the BALANCE between
the two peaks: a mean of 0.50 could mean 50% confident-positive songs
and 50% confident-negative songs, or it could mean all songs in the
uncertain middle at exactly 0.5. The album mean can't tell us which.

The 'top 5' and 'bottom 5' songs are songs the model is most confident
about — not 'the most negative' or 'the most positive'. A song with
bert_pos=0.000 isn't more negative than a song with bert_pos=0.001;
both are confidently negative. The model just disagrees on degree.

## Reproducing

```bash
python data/fetch_cots.py
python data/build_pipeline.py
python analyze/sentiment.py
```

Output files (gitignored, regenerable):
- `reports/sentiment_per_song.csv` — 244 rows × 14 sentiment columns
- `reports/sentiment_per_section.csv` — ~892 rows (one per song × tagged section)

**Reproducibility note**: `data/fetch_cots.py` downloads CoTS from
upstream's `main` branch HEAD. The CoTS Year column reports
Taylor's Version re-release years for Fearless, Red, 1989, and Speak
Now — `data/build_pipeline.py` now prefers album_meta.json year to
avoid this. For paper-grade reproducibility, pin CoTS to a specific
commit SHA + add a SHA256 check.
