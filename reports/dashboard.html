Taylor Swift's Songs — NLP Analysis

Phases 2-6 combined · 244 songs across 13 albums · CoTS v1.4 corpus

1. Career-long sentiment arc

Each point is a song. DistilBERT-SST2 positive probability — pretrained sentiment model, transferred to lyrics. Hover for song title and scores.

Headline (descriptive): Speak Now (2010) and 1989 (2014) average highest on DistilBERT pos; TTPD (2024) and Midnights (2022) lowest. The 'late-career breakup reckoning' interpretation is one reading of the data; the same shape is also consistent with 'slower songs get lower pos scores' or 'the model is more confident on short, hook-heavy lyrics.' Year axis uses canonical original-release years from album_meta.json (CoTS's year column reports Taylor's Version re-release years for Fearless/Red/1989/Speak Now).

2. Sentiment vs lexical diversity per album

Album-level mean DistilBERT pos (y) vs album-level mean MATTR-500 (x). MATTR-500 is moving-average type-token ratio — measures within-song vocabulary diversity, stable across song lengths.

Headline (descriptive, not causal): TTPD sits at the bottom-right (lowest DistilBERT pos + highest MATTR-500). Whether this is 'most linguistically and emotionally complex' is a value judgment, not a measurement — the chart only shows the relative position. Sample sizes per album are 12-31 songs; per-album means have standard errors of ~0.05-0.08 on the bert_pos scale.

3. Per-album section composition

% of total lyric characters per section, by album. Verses typically dominate (~50-60%), choruses ~15-20%, bridges ~10-15%, refrains & intros/outros small.

4. Per-section sentiment (phase 3)

Mean DistilBERT pos per section across all 244 songs. Counter-intuitively, bridges are the MOST positive section on DistilBERT pos (0.50), not the darkest. Pop-theory claim that bridges are darker than verses doesn't hold for Swift.

5. Vibe clusters (phase 6a — K-means)

K-means (K=10) on the 384-dim sentence embeddings. Each cluster shows which albums its songs come from.

Headline: 86% of songs have a nearest neighbor from a different album. Multiple explanations are possible (genuine theme sharing, common-English function words dominating the embedding, model limitations) — see the cross-album caveat in reports/similarity_summary.md. Within-album cluster consistency (how often a song's cluster-mates are from the same album) is highest for TTPD (97%), lowest for Life of a Showgirl (67%).

6. Top mutual nearest-neighbor pairs (phase 5)

Sentence-transformer (all-MiniLM-L6-v2) song embeddings, cosine similarity top-5 nearest neighbors. Mutual pairs = both A and B list each other in their top-5.

Headline: Top mutual pair is Back To December (Speak Now, 2010) ↔ 'Tis The Damn Season (Evermore, 2020) at 0.77. Both are quiet, regretful, winter songs about looking back.
