# Section-level sentiment analysis

Generated by `python analyze/section_analysis.py`. Reads the per-section CSV from `analyze/sentiment.py`.

**Notation**: `bert_pos` is DistilBERT-SST2 positive probability [0,1].
`vader_compound` and `tb_polarity` are rule-based [-1,1].

## 1. Per-section averages (all 244 songs, DistilBERT + rule-based)

| Section | n | VADER | TextBlob | DistilBERT pos |
|---------|---|-------|----------|---------------|
| verse | 244 | +0.251 | +0.090 | 0.409 |
| chorus | 243 | +0.354 | +0.100 | 0.370 |
| bridge | 221 | +0.195 | +0.077 | 0.499 |
| refrain | 9 | -0.013 | +0.043 | 0.359 |

**Reading**: Across all 244 songs, **bridges are the most positive section
on DistilBERT pos (0.499)**, followed by verses (0.409), then choruses
(0.370), then refrains (0.359). This inverts the standard pop-theory
expectation that bridges are darker than verses — in Swift's discography,
the bridge often functions as an emotional release or a hopeful turn
("I'd marry you with paper rings", "I'll be okay", etc.) rather than a
dark pivot.

Verses and choruses are surprisingly close on DistilBERT pos. On
VADER, choruses read as more positive (the hook words "love", "want",
"stay" cluster there); DistilBERT doesn't see that pattern.

Sample sizes: 244 verses, 243 choruses, 221 bridges, 9 refrains.
Intros/outros skipped (too few).

## 5. Bridge sentiment

221/244 songs have a tagged bridge section.

Of those, **101/221** (46%) have a bridge that's
darker (lower DistilBERT pos) than their verse. The classic
pop-theory claim that bridges are darker than verses is therefore
*not* the dominant pattern in Swift's writing — 54% of her bridges
are *more* positive than their verses. The song-level breakdown is
closer to a coin flip than a rule.

## Reproducing

```bash
python analyze/sentiment.py        # produces sentiment_per_section.csv
python analyze/section_analysis.py # this file
```

Note: this analyzer depends on DistilBERT being scored per-section,
which requires `analyze/sentiment.py` to be at the version that
extends phase B over each section (not just per-song).
