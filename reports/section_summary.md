# Section-level sentiment analysis

Generated by `python analyze/section_analysis.py`. Reads the per-section CSV from `analyze/sentiment.py`.

**Notation**: `bert_pos` is DistilBERT-SST2 positive probability [0,1].
`vader_compound` and `tb_polarity` are rule-based [-1,1].

## 1. Per-section averages (all 244 songs, DistilBERT + rule-based)

| Section | n | VADER | TextBlob | DistilBERT pos |
|---------|---|-------|----------|---------------|
| verse | 244 | +0.251 | +0.090 | 0.409 |
| chorus | 243 | +0.354 | +0.100 | 0.370 |
| bridge | 221 | +0.195 | +0.077 | 0.499 |
| refrain | 9 | -0.013 | +0.043 | 0.359 |

**Reading (what the data shows, not a theory)**: across all 244
songs, on DistilBERT pos the average bridge sits at 0.499 — the
highest of all sections. Verses average 0.409, choruses 0.370,
and refrains 0.359. Verses and choruses are close on DistilBERT
(0.409 vs 0.370) but diverge on VADER (verses +0.251 vs choruses
+0.354) — VADER reads choruses as the most positive section
because that's where hook words ('love', 'want', 'stay') cluster,
while DistilBERT doesn't see that lexical pattern as strongly.

**Caveat**: this is a single-corpus finding (Taylor Swift,
DistilBERT-SST2 fine-tuned on movie reviews). Calling it a
'pop-theory refutation' would overreach — the pattern could be
specific to her writing. We have no comparison corpus of other
songwriters to know.

## 5. Bridge sentiment

221/244 songs have a tagged bridge section.

Of those, **101/221** (46%) have a bridge that's
darker (lower DistilBERT pos) than their verse. Conversely,
120 (54%) have a bridge brighter
than their verse. The split is close to a coin flip on this
corpus — neither the 'bridge-darker' nor the 'bridge-brighter'
claim dominates.

Note: there is no single 'pop theory' that bridges are
necessarily darker. Different songwriting traditions treat
bridges differently (verse-chorus form often uses them as
tension-builders; some ballads use them as emotional release).
This dataset alone can't adjudicate which tradition Swift
follows most often — only that the average bridge sits higher
than the average verse on this particular sentiment signal.

## Reproducing

```bash
python analyze/sentiment.py        # produces sentiment_per_section.csv
python analyze/section_analysis.py # this file
```

Note: this analyzer depends on DistilBERT being scored per-section,
which requires `analyze/sentiment.py` to be at the version that
extends phase B over each section (not just per-song).
